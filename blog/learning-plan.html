Long term career goals

I wouldn’t say that I have a specific career goal. One of my personal goals/priorities in life is to write fiction, and part of the reason I’m learning to program is to find a career which might better accommodate my goals. Working remotely, and getting paid better were good incentives for me to enroll in this course. I’m not familiar enough with programming/coding to know what sort of careers or opportunities these skills might lead to, or whether I want a career in the industry, but I am optimistic and open minded. I’m interested in using the skills I learn in this course in various areas of my life, not just career. I can already see a lot of great potential for creative projects. I’m interested to hear from the perspective of others during this course, explore what a career in tech might look like!

Learning strengths and limitations.

My strengths are that I am a hard worker with a lot of persistence and optimism. I would say that my weaknesses are difficulties with relational logic, and getting sidetracked by new ideas. I enjoy puzzles, but find it difficult to intuitively understand complicated mathematical concepts. I expect to struggle with the more difficult technical parts of this course, but I’m confident in asking for help, and have already built a good relationship with my homegroup, some of whom are much more advanced than me, so hopefully they’ll be able to help if I get stuck.

What skills would I like to develop at Dev?

I’d like to learn to work collaboratively on projects with other people. I think that I have relatively good people skills, having worked in customer service for fifteen years, but I haven’t collaborated with many people in a professional context, and I think that’s a different set of skills. I would also like to prove to myself that I can do this, as it’s extremely outside of my usual comfort zone.

A commitment to workload management.

The first thing I’m going to do to manage my workload, is to frontload the beginning of my week with the core, technical stuff so that I can get a sense of how long it is taking me to understand the new concepts. This might look like working longer days on Monday to Wednesday, and if I’m running behind, I will have more of a buffer to catch up.

I’m going to keep up a good rapport with my cohort, particularly my homegroup, so that we can support each other throughout the process, and give each other help when we need it.

I am using a pig shaped egg timer (Rocksroy the pig) to measure my progress against the predicted time suggested for each exercise. I don’t always stick to these times, as I often have to revisit the lessons to make sure I understand, but it allows me to see how I am doing in relation to the weekly schedule.

When things are building up, I will go for a walk, or take a break of some kind. Alternatively, I have a few good friends who have established tech careers who have offered to call me/look at my code, whenever I need help or encouragement.

I expect the facilitation team to communicate important course information to us in a timely way, listen to our concerns if we have any, be available to help with technical issues (to a reasonable extent.)

The only scheduling conflicts I have are watching the premier league, Survivor, the UFC and feeding the cat twice a day. Everything else is secondary.
