MARGINS, BORDERS & PADDING


The internet is made of boxes

Any HTML element on a website can be a "box" A picture of a horse. A paragraph of text. An interactive element, like a newsletter sign up sheet.

But how do we arrange these "boxes" on the page?

We are going to explore MARGINS, BORDERS AND PADDING using the analogy of a painting/art gallery.




We can think of a webpage like the wall of an art gallery. Say we have a number of “paintings” we want to “hang” on the wall. Let's take a closer look at one of our boxes/paintings. In this analogy, the "painting" of the crocodile represents our element. The area between the element and the border is known as padding. The frame is what we call a border.
